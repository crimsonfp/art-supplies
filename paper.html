Although it may seem insignificant, paper actually plays a big role in art. The thickness, texture, and surface of the paper if meant specifically for different mediums. For example, watercolor paper and bristol are much thicker and have a slightly coarse surace texter, allowing the watery paint to seep into the paper, but not bleed through. Thick, smooth paper is best for hyper-realistic art, as textured paper prevents the pencil from acheiving an even shade because of the bumps. This leaves tiny yet obvious black spots, which are the opposite of what you're going for in realism. Thin printer paper is alright for unserious skecting, but a higher quality paper is reccomended for anything you might want to display.
