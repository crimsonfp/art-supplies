Colored pencils are similar to graphite pencils, yet they use wax to release pigment rather than graphite or lead. This is why they leave a smooth, waxy feel on paper after drawing. Because they're composed of wax, they also do not erase easily. However, you can somewhat color of them if you make a mistake, as long as you didn't press too hard originally. Colored pencils are popular for their easy control, persicion, ability to capture vibfrant color, belnding, and ability to harbor diverse artsyles. From cartoons to realism, colored pencils can do just about anything!
