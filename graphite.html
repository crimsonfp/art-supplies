Graphite is the main component in modern pencils. This is what gives them the ability to leave pigment on pages. Graphite limits a user to grayscale, but it is very easy to manipulate and add detailt to artwork. While lead used to be used in common pencils, this would cause lead poisoning. Graphite, on the other hand, is completely safe to use! It's easy to blend using a finger, a Q-tip, a blending stump, cloth, or even a shred of a paper towel. Tip: lean the penicl on it's side in order to get a wider and more even shade! You can also hand-sharpen your pencils in order to expose more of the graphite on all sides. Many professionals pefer this style of sharpening, as it allows for a wider variety of marks to be made. You can use these pencils for both drawing and writing, but make sure to pay attention to the HB scale! This controls how dark or light the pencil shades. The higher the number, the darker the pencil will shade with lighter pressure.
