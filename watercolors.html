Watercolors

Quality Watercolors.



Mijello // Daniel Smith // Schmincke // White Nights // Garbie


Watercolors are a very common art material. There is a large range of price and quality, and they are the most typically kind of paint for children to use, especially in art class. They allow for an incredibly unique type of color layering, make them hard for realism, but perfect for abstract, soft cartoons, childrens' book illustrations, and flowy artstyles. They're very easy to use and hard to waste, as any excess water will simply evaporate, leaving the pallet almost good as new. The downside of watercolors it the fact that you cannot mix them in the pallet, restricting your colors to those provided. However layering can be used to achieve unique colors not provided in the pallet. To clean your brushes, simply rinse them thoroughly but gently with water!
